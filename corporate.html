Design Elements

Please provide a list of design elements alongside their justifications:

  • Colour scheme:   
    • #8F7E62 The light brown signifies comfort.
    • #4C3921 The dark brown signifies comfort. 
    • #F3B23F The mustard yellow signifies adventure.
    • #95A387 The green signifies safety and nature.
    •  
  • Font choices: The font used for the headings as well as the body text  is Pangolin, a rounded and handwritten-style typeface that adds a personal, approachable feel to Campher’s branding. Its slightly playful curves and informal touch evoke warmth and authenticity, the key to building a trusting and safe environment for solo female travelers. Pangolin reflects empowerment with softness, embracing the balance between strength and approachability that defines Campher’s personality. Pangolin’s handwritten look also complements the brand’s emphasis on community and shared experiences. Overall, Pangolin reflects Campher’s core values: safety, comfort, empowerment and nature, wrapped in a warm, minimalistic visual language designed specifically for women travelers.
  • User interface patterns (e.g. grids, carousels, menu organizations etc.)
  • The CampHer Safety Tips page has a clean, user-centered layout grounded in familiar UI patterns and visual hierarchy principles. Using Bootstrap’s grid system, the page creates an intuitive and responsive two-column layout, text and images balanced side-by-side, enhancing readability and engagement. This alignment supports the F-pattern (Nielsen Norman Group), aligning with how users naturally scan digital content.
  • The structure of the navigation and content (e.g. how content units are distributed across pages)
  • Navigation structure follows a horizontal navbar pattern, a familiar UI design, which ensures quick access to core pages: Home, Experiences, Safety Tips, and Corporate. This consistent top-level navigation reduces cognitive load (Krug’s principle of “Don’t Make Me Think”) and supports predictable user behavior, allowing for fluid transitions between topics.
  • All these elements must be justified by referring to the theory and/or vocabulary of design
  • The content structure uses progressive disclosure: starting with a full-width image to anchor the page, followed by bold headlines, and  detailed, bullet-pointed safety advice. This method, backed by Gestalt principles (proximity and similarity), breaks information into digestible units, reducing overwhelm for users seeking practical advice.

    • How does website design fit the values and personality of the brand?
    • From a branding perspective, the website supports CampHer’s identity as a community-driven, safety-first platform for solo female campers. The color scheme (warm earth tones like #F3B23F), rounded images make the page feel approachable and empowering. The use of the Pangolin font adds a touch of personality, aligning with the brand's mix of comfort, care, and individuality.
    • How does website design fit the marketing and communication strategy?
    • In terms of marketing and communication, the page is structured to guide users through a funnel: education → trust → action. For instance, after providing detailed safety tips, the final call-to-action (“Join our community”) appears with standout coloring and strategic whitespace. This aligns with AIDA principles (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action), nudging users toward engagement without aggressive persuasion.
    • How does website design help showcase the unique value proposal of the product?
    • The website reinforces CampHer’s unique value proposition—empowering solo female van campers through shared knowledge and community. Instead of selling gear or services, the site offers emotional value: safety, solidarity, and peace of mind. The content tone is conversational yet informative, reflecting CampHer’s voice as protective yet empowering.

Testing Report

  • The primary goal of our testing is to evaluate how effectively the website communicates its purpose and supports the needs of our target audience: solo female travelers interested in camper van adventures. We want to understand whether users can easily navigate the site and access important features such as safety information, campsite suggestions, and community spaces. Additionally, we aim to assess if the visual design and overall tone of the website create a sense of trust, empowerment, and comfort. Finally, we hope to identify any usability issues or moments of confusion that could restrict the overall user experience, so we can refine and improve the platform accordingly.
  • To evaluate the usability and visual coherence of our website prototype, we conducted a user testing session with four participants. Three of them aligned well with our target audience: independent, safety-conscious women interested in van travel. One participant, although male and therefore outside our core demographic, still provided valuable insights from a UX (user experience) perspective, which we found extremely helpful in refining functionality. The testing took place on campus in a casual, focused setting, where participants were asked to interact with our paper-based prototype of the Campher website. We provided clear instructions, asking them to share their thoughts and impressions out loud while exploring the site. We guided them to reflect on key aspects such as content clarity, navigation flow, accessibility, and visual appeal. These sessions were audio recorded to ensure we could review their feedback in detail later. Overall, the responses were very positive, confirming that our design direction resonated with users. However, one participant made a particularly insightful observation: she suggested that the navigation bar (originally designed as a hidden vertical menu) would be more intuitive and user-friendly if placed horizontally and kept visible at all times at the top of the page. We took this feedback seriously and implemented the change to improve the overall navigation experience. This testing phase confirmed that our website design is not only aligned with our brand’s values of safety, ease, and empowerment, but also responsive to the real needs of our users.

Management

Lean Canvas

Problem

    1. Safety concerns while camping alone as a woman;
    2. Lack of clear, reliable information about campsite locations;
    3. Limited emergency communication options during travel.
  • Current alternatives for women interested in solo camping include blog posts, general camping apps, and social media communities. However, these sources often lack credibility, verified safety information, and a focus on the specific needs of female travelers. While platforms like Google Maps and national park websites do provide campsite details, they do not cater specifically to the concerns and safety priorities of solo female campers.

Solution

  • A user-friendly digital platform like Campher that provides curated safety tips, verified camping locations, and a supportive community space. 
  • A growing network where users can share real-time safety updates, ask questions, and receive expert advice from experienced solo travelers tailored for solo female travelers.

Customer segments

  • Millennial and Gen Z women who love nature, camping, and road travel. Solo female travelers who prioritize independence, safety, and sustainable travel practices.
  • Our ideal users are tech-savvy, eco-conscious, and safety-aware women. While some early adopters may already have solo travel experience and are now seeking more autonomy and a supportive community, Campher is equally designed for beginners and those who are just starting to explore the idea of vanlife. Whether they're seasoned adventurers or simply curious about taking their first steps into solo camping, Campher offers the tools, resources, and encouragement to make that journey safe, empowering, and accessible.

Unique value proposition

  • Campher is the first platform designed to empower and protect solo female travelers by combining verified safety resources, curated gear recommendations, and a supportive community, making vanlife not just possible, but safe and inspiring for women at every stage of their journey.

Unfair advantage

  • Our brand niche focus on female safety in camping, which makes it both highly relevant and emotionally resonant.
  • Our brand aims to reach a growing, trust-driven community and partnerships with female vanlifers and travel influencers that cannot be easily replicated by generic camping platforms.  

Channels

  • Our main path to costumers are Instagram and Facebook, for community-building, storytelling, and brand presence.
  • Our website as the main interaction point for features, resources, and memberships.

Services/products

  • Campher is a digital platform featuring verified campsite listings, real-time safety updates, a user forum and a community hub, educational content such as safety guides and travel tips, as well as premium memberships and event offerings. It has a strong alignment with values like empowerment, safety, accessibility, and nature. Validation of Assumptions

The name Campher fuses “Camper” with “Her”, instantly conveying its female-centered mission while remaining playful and easy to remember. In comparison to existing platforms like "Hipcamp" or "Park4Night", Campher stands out by being specifically tailored to women and by offering a community-driven support system instead of focusing solely on campsite listings. The goddess compass logo further enhances the brand’s differentiation by visually representing guidance and feminine empowerment, creating a strong emotional connection with the target audience.
