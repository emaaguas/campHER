Design Elements

Please provide a list of design elements alongside their justifications:

  • Colour scheme:   
    • #8F7E62 The light brown signifies comfort.
    • #4C3921 The dark brown signifies comfort. 
    • #F3B23F The mustard yellow signifies adventure.
    • #95A387 The green signifies safety and nature.
    •  
  • Font choices: The font used for the headings as well as the body text  is Pangolin, a rounded and handwritten-style typeface that adds a personal, approachable feel to Campher’s branding. Its slightly playful curves and informal touch evoke warmth and authenticity, the key to building a trusting and safe environment for solo female travelers. Pangolin reflects empowerment with softness, embracing the balance between strength and approachability that defines Campher’s personality. Pangolin’s handwritten look also complements the brand’s emphasis on community and shared experiences. Overall, Pangolin reflects Campher’s core values: safety, comfort, empowerment and nature, wrapped in a warm, minimalistic visual language .
  • The Campher website uses clean and intuitive design patterns that contribute to a smooth and user-friendly experience. A fixed horizontal navigation bar is positioned at the top of the page, providing quick access to key sections such as Experiences, Safety Tips, and Corporate. Keeping navigation consistent across the site minimizes confusion and makes it easier for visitors to explore content without feeling lost. The overall layout follows a grid-based structure, creating a clear sense of visual order. On the home page the content is organized into well-defined sections, like the “About Us” text next to the logo, or the login form placed alongside the vision statement. This type of layout helps users naturally group related content and understand the hierarchy of information on the page. A large image carousel is used across the homepage and “Experiences” section to visually communicate the brand’s atmosphere. These images do more than decorate, they immediately establish a sense of calm, freedom, and empowerment, which are key aspects of Campher’s identity. Carousels also serve a functional purpose, allowing multiple visuals to be displayed without overwhelming the user. Interactive components such as the login box, chat button, and testimonial comment sections keeps interactive areas clean, focused, and easy to use. These elements clearly guide users toward meaningful actions, like signing up, asking for help, or sharing their experiences. The visual language of the website is carefully aligned with Campher’s values and personality. The use of a soft, earthy color palette creates a natural and calming atmosphere, reflecting the brand’s connection to nature and its focus on comfort and safety. Rounded typography and smooth design elements contribute to a welcoming tone, which supports the brand’s message of accessibility and inclusiveness. Important values like Safety, Nature, Community, and Easy Access are not only embedded in the site’s content but also visually highlighted in their own section. This reinforces the message that these aren’t background ideas, they’re central to what Campher offers. The site design also complements the brand’s marketing and communication goals. It invites engagement through clearly placed calls to action, such as “Join the community” and “Start a chat”. Using real testimonials makes the website feel more genuine and helps build trust, especially for an audience that values community and shared experiences. The website's clear structure also leaves room for future content, such as workshops, product features, or brand partnerships, without needing to change the design. This design helps Campher stand out as more than a resource, it becomes a companion and guide for women entering or continuing their solo traveling journey.
  • The CampHer Safety Tips page has a clean, user-centered layout. Using Bootstrap’s grid system, the page creates an intuitive and responsive two-column layout, text and images balanced side-by-side, enhancing readability and engagement.
  • Navigation structure follows a horizontal navigation bar pattern, which ensures quick access to core pages: Home, Experiences, Safety Tips, and Corporate.
    • From a branding perspective, the website supports CampHer’s identity as a community-driven, safety-first platform for solo female campers. The color scheme (warm earth tones), rounded images make the page feel approachable and empowering.     Testing Report

    • The primary goal of our testing is to evaluate how effectively the website communicates its purpose and supports the needs of our target audience: solo female travelers interested in camper van adventures. We want to understand whether users can easily navigate the site and access important features such as safety information, campsite suggestions, and community spaces. Additionally, we aim to assess if the visual design and overall tone of the website create a sense of trust, empowerment, and comfort. Finally, we hope to identify any usability issues or moments of confusion that could restrict the overall user experience, so we can refine and improve the platform accordingly.
    • To evaluate the usability and visual coherence of our website prototype, we conducted a user testing session with four participants. Three of them aligned well with our target audience: independent, safety-conscious women interested in van travel. One participant, although male and therefore outside our core demographic, still provided valuable insights from a UX (user experience) perspective, which we found extremely helpful in refining functionality. The testing took place on campus in a casual, focused setting, where participants were asked to interact with our paper-based prototype of the Campher website. We provided clear instructions, asking them to share their thoughts and impressions out loud while exploring the site. We guided them to reflect on key aspects such as content clarity, navigation flow, accessibility, and visual appeal. These sessions were audio recorded to ensure we could review their feedback in detail later. Overall, the responses were very positive, confirming that our design direction resonated with users. However, one participant made a particularly insightful observation: she suggested that the navigation bar (originally designed as a hidden vertical menu) would be more intuitive and user-friendly if placed horizontally and kept visible at all times at the top of the page. We took this feedback seriously and implemented the change to improve the overall navigation experience. This testing phase confirmed that our website design is not only aligned with our brand’s values of safety, ease, and empowerment, but also responsive to the real needs of our users.

    Marketing

    Context of campaign and promotional activities

    The Campher campaign was designed to promote a safe, empowering space for solo female van campers. The message focused on normalizing solo travel for women, while emphasizing safety, preparation, and confidence. The aim was to engage and connect with Gen Z and Millennial women by using digital storytelling and community interaction across key platforms. Our promotional activities were primarily executed on Instagram and Facebook, based on audience research from our Communication Media Plan. These platforms were chosen because they are where our audience is most active and engaged, particularly when it comes to lifestyle, travel, and wellness content. The campaign included regular feed posts twice a week (Tuesdays and Fridays), and daily Instagram stories featuring behind-the-scenes content, safety tips, polls, and interactive elements. The objectives, as outlined in our Communication Media Plan, were to:

    • Reach at least 400 Instagram profiles;
    • Receive 20+ likes per post with a few meaningful comments;
    • Start building a loyal and interactive community;
    • Consistently post 2x per week and maintain daily story activity.

    Learning Points

    Throughout the project, we gained valuable insights into social media marketing, particularly within a niche audience. One of the key takeaways was that growing a following takes more time and strategy than expected. Even though we stayed consistent with posting and maintained strong visual branding, overall engagement (likes, comments, shares) was lower than we anticipated. On the positive side, our message and visual identity were clear and well-received. Users who came across our content understood Campher’s mission and appreciated the tone and purpose. However, it became clear that visibility alone is not enough, relying only on posts didn’t generate the reach or interaction we hoped for. If we were to do it again, we would combine posts with more active outreach, including influencer collaboration, paid ads, and more interactive formats. We also realized the importance of creating content that encourages users to participate, not just consume.

    Future Planning

    If the project were to continue, our next step would be to expand reach and boost engagement using a more strategic approach. Based on our experience and what we learned, we would:

    • Collaborate with influencers and vanlife communities to gain trust and improve visibility within our niche;
    • Use paid promotions on Instagram and Facebook to target a wider and more specific audience;
    • Shift content from purely informative to a more interactive mix including polls, quizzes, user-generated content, and safety-related challenges or prompts;
    • Create short-form video content (e.g. Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts) to present safety tips in a fun, shareable, and relatable format;
    • Partner with outdoor brands (e.g. camping gear, travel safety companies) for cross-promotions and giveaways to increase interest and credibility.

    Professionalism

    All of our campaign content was developed with care to reflect Campher’s brand identity. The images used in feed posts and on the site are high-quality, consistent in tone, and visually aligned with our earthy, nature-focused palette. Our posts maintained strong readability and coherence, using fonts and layouts that matched the brand's friendly and professional voice.

    Lean Canvas

    Problem

    Through the early-stage interviews made with potential users, we identified these key challenges:

    • Limited access to secure and well-reviewed camping sites;
    •  Lack of built-in, female-focused security tools or campground infrastructure;
    •  Limited emergency communication options during travel;
    •  Emotional stress reduces the joy of solo camping;
    •  Fear of isolation and unknown people, especially at night;
    •  Theft concerns due to valuables in vans;
    • Inadequate ventilation solutions - safety compromised when windows stay open.  

    This were the problems faced by our target group, which we solved by giving them a platform (CampHer) where we share safety tips and give a space for campers and aspiring campers to communicate with each other, share their experience, and create a community.

    The interviews findings are available here: Interview findings

    Solution

    • A curated platform with a verified database of safe campsites, parking areas, and emergency services;
    • Safety checklist tools, reviewed spots and real-time updates;
    • Guides on non-violent safety strategies like decoys, alarms, lighting;
    • A community chat to locate and connect with other female campers nearby.

    By offering our target audience a space on our page where they can share and see the different and various user experiences on camping, Campher helps them feeling more safe while camping alone and ends the lack of information about camping site locations, and gives another emergency coommunication option.

    Customer segments

    • Our ideal customer would be a millennial/Gen Z woman that works remotely, and has embraced the camper van life. Someone that likes to be connected to nature and aspires to find a community that gives her van camping tips and makes her feel safe when camping alone.
    • Our target group are people that love spending time in nature; millennial and Gen Z women who enjoy camping and van life; solo female traveler's seeking a safe and reliable camping experience; environmentally conscious traveler's who value sustainable outdoor practices; women who want freedom, but not fear.

    Unique value proposition

    CampHer is:

    • a platform dedicated to ensuring safety and security for female campers;
    • a platform with curated resources, gear recommendations, and community support for solo travelers';
    • a platform with database of verified safe camping locations, parking spots, and emergency resources;
    • a platform that gives combining real-life safety hacks, expert-vetted locations, and a growing community of road-savvy travelers;
    • a platform that gives combining real-life safety hacks, expertly picked locations, and a growing community of travelers;
    • the only platform focusing on solo female campers’ safety;
    • a beautifully designed, intuitive experience that builds trust.

    Unfair advantage

    • While most camper van rental platforms are gender-neutral, Campher builds safety into the core for women, which requires deep understanding, trust, and specific information, not easily replicated without credibility;
    • Personalised travel planning for solo female traveler's demands specialised knowledge and real community insight into women's travel needs, far beyond generic route planning tools;
    • Having community features for the female users to create a trusted, closed, and safe network requires time, vetting systems, and a strong brand ethos. Competitors can't fake a loyal, secure female-led community overnight;
    •  Our trusted network of female-friendly campsites and locations is built from user feedback, others would need years of experience and relationships to match it;
    • The authenticity and emotional connection brought through real stories create a powerful bond with the audience that can’t be replicated with generic marketing;
