Design Elements

 

Colour Scheme
  • #8F7E62 - The light brown signifies comfort, evokes the grounded, earthy lifestyle of van life. Creates a calming and stable visual atmosphere, aligning with the feeling of comfort and reliability that solo female travelers seek.
  • #4C3921 - The dark brown signifies warmth, reliability, and a grounded connection to nature. This deep, earthy tone creates a sense of comfort and stability as well, values that are essential for solo female travelers seeking safety and reassurance on the road. It serves as a strong foundation in the color palette, anchoring the design with a trustworthy and mature visual tone.
  • #F3B23F - The mustard yellow signifies adventure and curiosity. It energizes without being overwhelming, adds a bold and a positive accent that encourages action and exploration.
  • #95A387 - The green signifies safety, calm, and a deep connection to nature. This muted green creates a peaceful atmosphere that aligns with the tranquil mindset many travelers seek. It reinforces the brand’s message of eco-consciousness and mindfulness, while also supporting a feeling of balance and well-being.
  •  

    • Campher is a brand created for Millennial and Gen Z women who value safety, nature, and community while traveling solo in camper vans. Our design language reflects these exact core values through soft but empowering visuals, intuitive UI choices, and a consistent focus on emotional connection and trust with our target audience. During our website testings the users were able to identify the brands purpose just by seeing the page’s design. This imidiate recognizition confirmed to our team that CampHer’s design successfully aligns with the brand’s target audience and identity.

     

    Font Choices
    • The font used both for headings and body text is Pangolin, a rounded, handwritten-style typeface that adds a personal and approachable feel to CampHer’s branding. Its slightly playful curves and informal touch evoke warmth and authenticity, which are qualities essential to building a trusting and safe environment for solo female travelers.
    • Pangolin communicates empowerment with softness, reflecting the balance between independence and comfort that defines Campher’s personality. This makes it especially suited for our target audience of Millennial and Gen Z women who value both strength and safety in their solo travel experiences. The handwritten aesthetic of Pangolin reinforces the brand’s community-driven nature and adds a human, friendly tone to the interface. It mirrors the way travelers might journal their experiences, making the digital space feel more like a personal companion and community than a product.
    • Choosing a single font throughout the website was an intentional decision to maintain visual clarity and cohesion. By avoiding multiple font styles, we reduce distraction and help users focus on the content, enhancing usability and reinforcing CampHer’s minimalistic, user-centered design approach.

     

    User Interface Patterns and Layout
    • Fixed Top Navigation Bar: A persistent horizontal menu ensures logical access to all major sections such as Home, Experiences, Safety Tips, and Corporate. This pattern was chosen to minimize cognitive load and enhance user orientation, especially on mobile devices where constant access to navigation is key.
    • Grid-Based Layouts: The website uses a responsive grid structure (particularly via Bootstrap) to create a clear visual hierarchy and maintain consistent spacing between elements. This not only improves readability and scannability, but also helps users intuitively group related content, such as pairing testimonials with imagery or safety tips with icons.
    • Image Carousels: Featured prominently on the homepage page, the carousels serve both emotional and functional roles. It creates an immersive, atmospheric introduction to Campher’s website while preventing content overload by allowing multiple visuals to rotate in limited space.
    • Interactive Components: Elements like the chat button, login form, and testimonials section are designed to focus the user’s attention on meaningful actions.
    • Buttons and images: Rounded UI elements (e.g., buttons, image frames) visually express inclusiveness and reduce harshness, which resonates with an audience that values emotional safety and approachability. Real user stories and natural imagery create emotional credibility and trust, which is vital for a brand supporting female solo travelers. Testimonials also serve as social proof, reinforcing the sense of community.

     

    Brand strategy

    From a content perspective, the site's tone, font, and layout support storytelling and user engagement. The use of personal stories and warm, relatable language in the headers and testimonials helps communicate the brand's message in a more genuine and engaging way.

    The call-to-action structure (“Join the community,” “Start a chat”) is intentionally placed after emotionally resonant content. We used this marketing tatic to get the users to interact and get involved with the community.

    The visual strategy complements Campher’s lean canvas, focusing on community-building as a key value proposition. Elements like user photos and chat features directly reflect the brand’s mission to foster connection and empowerment.

    Testing Report

    The objective of the website testing was to evaluate:

    • How clearly the site communicates Campher’s brand purpose.
    • Whether users could complete key tasks (e.g., explore experiences, find safety tips).
    • How engaging and intuitive the navigation and visual structure felt.
     

    For our testing we had 4 women from the target demographic participating (Millennial/Gen Z solo travelers):

    • Female, 19 years old;
    • Female, 20 years old;
    • Female, 21years old;
    • Female, 21years old.
     

    All the website testings took place in a calm and comfortable environment. There the participants were asked to:

    1. Find and read a user experience on the “Experiences” page.
    2. Locate safety advice for first-time solo travelers.
    3. Navigate back to the homepage.
    4. Sign up or engage with the “Join the Community” section.
    5. Describe what they believe the brand offers after viewing the homepage.
     

    Findings from the website testings:

    • There was no rating on the experiences page, which made it hard to capture the users attention into reading the experiences, and makes it hard for the user to recognize the urgency and importance of the shared experience right away.
    • There is no searching bar in the experiences page which makes it hard for users to explore for specific experiences or doubts with key words.
    • The home page doesn’t transmit the brand’s message amd purpose right away in a clear way;
    • Thers no automatic “go bac to the top” button on the experiences page, which makes it harder and longer for the users to go back to the navuigation bar at the top;
    • The safety tips page is not inviting enough which make the users loose interest on the information there shared.
     

    Future possible improvements:

    1. Redesign the homepage intro section to instantly communicate the brand’s purpose using a concise mission statement and powerful image. First impressions matter most for user engagement and brand clarity.
    2. Add a rating or tagging system to the Experiences page to prioritize impactful or trending stories. This will improve content discoverability and emotional relevance. It helps users find valuable content and boosts engagement.
    3. Implement a search bar with keyword support to help users find relevant experiences or safety advice more easily.
    4. Redesign the Safety Tips page with visual elements (icons, infographics, user quotes) to make the content more engaging and scannable. It improves both readability and user retention of important info.
    5. Include a floating “back to top” button for long-scroll pages (e.g. Experiences page) to improve usability, especially on mobile.

    Marketing

    Context of campaign and promotional activities

    CampHer is a campaign created to promote a safe, empowering, and community-driven environment for solo female van campers. The brand’s message centers on normalizing solo travel for women, while emphasizing safety, preparedness, and confidence. It speaks directly to a niche but growing community of Gen Z and Millennial women passionate about vanlife, independence, and nature-based wellness.

    Based on audience insights from our Communication Media Plan, our promotional strategy focused on Instagram and Facebook (the platforms where our target audience is most active). These channels are especially relevant for lifestyle, travel, and wellness content, making them ideal for building emotional resonance and community around the Campher brand.

    We posted twice a week on the Instagram feed and maintained daily story content, including:

    • User experiences
    • Safety tips
    • Informational videos
    • Interactive engagement elements like questions
     
    Objectives

    As defined in our Communication Media Plan, our goals for the campaign were to:

    • Reach 400+ Instagram profiles during the campaign period
    • Achieve 15+ likes per post, with meaningful comment interactions
    • Start building a loyal and interactive community
    • Maintain a consistent content schedule
     
    Strategy: How We Communicated Our Message

    The campaign was designed to align with the values and digital behaviors of our audience by structuring our content in three key phases:

    1. Aspirational Content (Inspiration + Storytelling)
      • Photos and quotes that highlighted the beauty and freedom of solo travel
      • Real-life testimonials of female vanlifers
      • Aimed at emotional resonance and brand lifestyle representation
    2. Educational Content (Safety + Empowerment)
      • Short guides, safety checklists, and tips for first-time solo campers
      • Clear, informative posts formatted for easy sharing
      • Purpose: Build trust, establish authority, and support women practically
    3. Interactive and Community-Driven Content (Engagement)
      • Polls, “This or That” games, and Question ans Answer boxes on Instagram Stories
      • Encouraged participation and personal sharing
      • Helped identify what our audience cares about most
       

    This phased approach allowed us to develop familiarity, trust, and engagement with our audience, which helps on slowly building a sense of shared identity.

    Learning Points

    What Worked:

    1. Strong Visual Identity- The use of custom illustrations and cohesive color palettes immediately differentiated Campher from typical travel accounts. This unique aesthetic was consistently understood and appreciated by our followers, helping build instant brand recognition.
    2. Clear Messaging and Mission Alignment- Followers quickly grasped Campher’s tone and purpose — safety, empowerment, and freedom in solo female travel. Our content reinforced this narrative across all formats, from feed posts to story engagement.
    3. High Engagement on Key Posts- Our highest-performing reel (490 views) showed that short-form, relatable content has the potential to outperform static visuals. This confirms a strong opportunity in video-based storytelling.
    4. Emotional Resonance through Music and Atmosphere- Pairing posts with carefully selected, travel-themed music added an immersive quality to our content, making it more engaging and emotionally grounded for our audience.
     

    What Didn’t Go as Expected:

    1. Limited Organic Reach- Relying solely on unpaid content meant we couldn’t break out of the algorithm’s initial limitations. Although we reached 401 accounts, the pace of follower growth and reach plateaued quickly.
    2. Lower-than-Expected Facebook Engagement- Despite having a presence, our Facebook page didn’t generate meaningful interaction. This suggests our audience is more active on Instagram, or that the content wasn’t tailored well for the platform.
    3. Passive Interaction Patterns- While our content received likes and views, it inspired limited two-way communication. Polls and story features were underutilized by followers, indicating a need for stronger community-building tactics.

    Future Planning

    To grow both our reach and engagement, we recommend the following strategic shifts:

    • Invest in Paid Promotion- Running small, targeted ad campaigns on Instagram and Facebook can help us reach highly specific audiences (e.g. women aged 20–35 interested in vanlife, travel, and wellness). This would boost visibility beyond our immediate network.
    • Influencer and Micro-Creator Collaborations- Partnering with female vanlifers, content creators, or safety advocates could amplify trust and introduce Campher to an established community. This adds authenticity and can increase interaction.
    • Expand Video-Based Content- Building on the success of our top-performing reel, we would create more short-form videos such as:
      • “3 Solo Travel Safety Tips”
      • “Campervan Morning Routines”
      • “What I Pack for a Weekend Off-Grid”

        • Platforms: Instagram Reels, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts.

    • Clearer, Action-Driven Captions- Including specific CTAs in every post such as:
      • “Tag someone who’d love this”
      • “Share your camper setup with #MyCampherSpace”
      • “Save this tip for later!”

        • …can prompt more active audience responses.

    • Interactive and Gamified- Community Content Introducing weekly themes or challenges such as:
      • #SoloSundayStory: share a solo travel experience
      • #CampHERTips: followers submit their own hacks or advice
      • Instagram Question and Answers with real vanlifers

        • These would position Campher not just as a brand, but as a lifestyle platform.

    While the initial campaign was a strong foundation in establishing brand identity and trust, the next phase should emphasize growth, engagement, and community-building. With refined tactics, including influencer partnerships, interactive storytelling, and a cross-platform video strategy, CampHer can grow from a campaign into a real movement, empowering more women to travel solo, safely, and confidently.

     

    Professionalism

    In our Campher campaign, we intentionally chose a non-traditional, illustration-based visual style to set ourselves apart from typical travel or lifestyle campaigns that rely heavily on real-life photography. This choice was made to spark curiosity, visually stand out, and create a softer, more emotionally engaging brand image.

    In line with recent research, our campaign for Campher leveraged custom digital illustrations to create a unique, recognizable, and emotionally resonant brand identity. According to the study “The Role of Digital Illustrations in Communicating Brand Identity” (2023), digital illustrations help brands express tone, values, and personality in a way that is visually cohesive and emotionally impactful, particularly across digital platforms.

    The paper emphasizes that illustrations enable brands to:

    • Create stronger emotional associations through symbolic and stylized visuals
    • Increase differentiation in saturated visual environments (such as Instagram)
    • Build trust and relatability with audiences by offering a “hand-crafted” or humanized aesthetic

    This directly supports our approach: rather than relying on conventional lifestyle photography, we used illustration to convey safety, creativity, and empowerment — aligning with Campher’s mission to support solo female travelers. The illustrations allowed us to represent abstract values (like independence and self-trust) in a visual form that felt both warm and approachable.

    Lean Canvas

     
    Problem

    Through the early-stage interviews made with potential users, we identified these key challenges:

    • Limited access to secure and well-reviewed camping sites;
    •  Lack of built-in, female-focused security tools or campground infrastructure;
    •  Limited emergency communication options during travel;
    •  Emotional stress reduces the joy of solo camping;
    •  Fear of isolation and unknown people, especially at night;
    •  Theft concerns due to valuables in vans;
    • Inadequate ventilation solutions - safety compromised when windows stay open.  

    This were the problems faced by our target group, which we solved by giving them a platform (CampHer) where we share safety tips and give a space for campers and aspiring campers to communicate with each other, share their experience, and create a community.

    The interviews findings are available here: Interview findings

     
    Solution
    • A curated platform with a verified database of safe campsites, parking areas, and emergency services;
    • Safety checklist tools, reviewed spots and real-time updates;
    • Guides on non-violent safety strategies like decoys, alarms, lighting;
    • A community chat to locate and connect with other female campers nearby.

    By offering our target audience a space on our page where they can share and see the different and various user experiences on camping, Campher helps them feeling more safe while camping alone and ends the lack of information about camping site locations, and gives another emergency coommunication option.

    Validation of Assumptions

      Assumption 1:

    • Solo female travelers want to feel safe and emotionally supported, not just informed.

      Validation:

    • Through feedback on our Instagram posts and problem interviews, we found that safety and emotional reassurance were top priorities. Many respondents expressed anxiety around solo travel and valued calm, relatable content over highly stylized or adrenaline-filled posts. This insight shaped our tone of voice, our visual style, and the focus on safety tips, mood-setting content, and positive messaging throughout the campaign.

     

      Assumption 2:

    • Our target audience prefers visual, emotionally resonant content over detailed, text-heavy advice.

      Validation:

    • We noticed that our illustrated posts and reels — especially those paired with relaxing music — outperformed longer informational captions or resource-style posts. The photo collage format and stylized graphics resonated particularly well, indicating a preference for digestible, aesthetically pleasing content. This validated our decision to use illustrations and storytelling as core elements of Campher’s content strategy.

     

      Assumption 3:

    • Women exploring vanlife want a sense of community and representation in online spaces.

      Validation:

    • Interviews revealed that many women felt underrepresented in the broader vanlife content space, which is often male-dominated or influencer-focused. They appreciated seeing content that reflected their own interests — calm routines, safety guidance, solo activities — and not just extreme adventure. This validated Campher’s positioning as a relatable, inclusive digital space.

     
    Validation of Visual Identity and Brand Name

    The name CampHer fuses “Camper” with “Her”, presenting its female-centered mission while remaining playful and easy to remember. In comparison to existing platforms like "Hipcamp" or "Park4Night", CampHer stands out by being specifically tailored to women and by offering a community-driven support system instead of focusing solely on campsite listings. The goddess compass logo further enhances the brand’s differentiation by visually representing guidance and feminine empowerment, creating a strong emotional connection with the target audience.

    Our visual identity uses warm earth tones, soft lines, and custom illustrations that evoke safety, creativity, and freedom, aligning with our values and setting us apart from more rugged or influencer-driven vanlife content. Every design decision, from post layout to music choice, reinforces a feeling of gentle empowerment and emotional clarity.
