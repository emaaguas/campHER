Marit, 29 – Utrecht, NL 

I’ve always loved the idea of freedom that comes with vanlife—just me, the road, and nature. But the fear of being alone, especially as a woman, kept holding me back. I didn’t know where to start or how to keep myself safe on the road. Then I found Campher, and it truly changed everything. Their safety resources are not only practical but also written in a way that makes you feel supported, not scared. From campsite recommendations to tips on locking your van securely, the site covers everything I was worried about—and even things I hadn’t thought of yet. I took my first solo trip last spring, and I used their checklist religiously. Knowing I had a trusted source to turn to gave me the confidence to follow my dream. I now feel independent and empowered in a way I never expected. Thank you, Campher, for helping me feel safe and strong.

Sofia, 24 – Breda, NL 

When I started researching vanlife, I found so many blogs and Instagram posts—but none of them really spoke to the experience of being a young woman traveling alone. I wanted more than pretty photos; I wanted honesty, community, and practical advice. That’s when I discovered Campher. What stood out to me most was how real it felt. Their stories from other women made me feel understood and seen. It wasn’t just about gear or travel hacks—it was about mindset, emotional strength, and learning to trust myself. The section on staying mentally grounded while solo traveling hit home for me. It reminded me that being alone doesn’t mean being lonely—it can mean being free. Now I travel on weekends in my converted van and use Campher’s tips before every trip. From finding safe parking spots to listening to music that soothes my nerves at night, their resources are part of my journey. Campher helped me believe that I belong in this space too.

Emma, 32 – Groningen, NL 

Before Campher, vanlife was a dream I kept putting off. As a woman, I had so many ‘what ifs’ running through my head—what if I get stuck in the wrong area, what if something breaks, what if I’m not cut out for this? I found Campher through a friend and started exploring their website, and wow… it was like a deep breath of fresh air. Their detailed guides on solo travel, the safety advice tailored specifically to women, and the empowering language throughout gave me a whole new perspective. They didn’t just tell me I could do it—they showed me how. I especially loved the part of the website that breaks down planning a trip: legal spots to park, emergency contacts, self-defense tools, and even how to mentally prepare for a solo adventure. Thanks to Campher, I’ve now completed my first two solo trips—one to the coast and one to the Veluwe. I didn’t just learn how to do it safely—I learned how to enjoy it. Their mission of support, safety, and empowerment shines through, and I’m so proud to be part of the community they’re building.
